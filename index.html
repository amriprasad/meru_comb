Meru Combinatorics 2023
ABOUT THE CONFERENCE

This is the first of a series of annual conferences on combinatorics in India: the Meru Annual Combinatorics Conference.

Meru stands for the sacred mountain of ancient Indian cosmology, and also for the triangle of binomial coefficients studied by Indian prosodists.

Dates: 28 May to 1 June, 2023

Venue: Pondicherry University

Contributed Talks
Niranjan Balachandran, IIT Bombay.

Set Systems with Restricted Intersections

Consider the following extremal combinatorial problem. Supopse $K\subset\mathbb{N}$ and let $\mathcal{F}\subset 2^{[n]}$ be a family of sets such that for any distinct $A,B\in\mathcal{F}$, we have $|A\cap B|\in K$. How large can $|\mathcal{F}|$ be? As it turns out, this problem has its roots in design theory, and later spawned several other problems of the same prototype, with further refinements, finite vector field analogues, and so on, and ushered in a variety of combinatorial techniques and problems. Recently, we introduced a fractional variant of the same problem. This version also seems a robust notion in the sense that it has also spawned several variants, and also brought in a variety of other problems, with many of them still not settled completely. In these lectures, we shall first take a bird's-eye-view of the classical problem (and the related variants) before delving into this recent fractional variant, and present 3 recent results. (Based on joint work with several co-authors including Srimanta Bhattacharya, Krishn Kher, Rogers Mathew, Tapas Mishra, and Brahadeesh Sankarnarayanan)

Santosha Kumar Pattanayak, IIT Kanpur.

Adventures in Schubert Calculus

In this short course we motivate and develop the basic machinery of Schubert calculus as a method for counting the number of subspaces of a particular dimension intersecting certain given subspaces, an important problem historically in enumerative geometry. After introducing basic objects of study such as Schubert cells and Schubert varieties in the Grassmannian and flag varieties, we discuss how intersections of these varieties can express the motivating enumerative questions. We discuss algebraic and geometric properties of these objects and see how properties of these objects can be interpreted algebraically using cohomology. We then discuss symmetric function theory in order to establish an isomorphism from the cohomology ring of the Grassmannian to a quotient of the ring of symmetric functions. We discuss the Duality theorem, Pieri's formula, Giambell's formula and finally the Littlewood-Richardson rule for multiplying arbitrary Schur polynomials, which via the aforementioned isomorphism allows us to compute in the cohomology ring - thus solving our original enumerative geometry problems.
